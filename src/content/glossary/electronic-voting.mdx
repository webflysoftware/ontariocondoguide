## In plain language

**Electronic voting** lets owners vote online or through a meeting platform instead of marking paper ballots. It can improve access for remote owners and speed tabulation — but only when the corporation has proper authority in its **declaration**, **bylaws**, and meeting notice.

Electronic voting is not automatic in every Ontario condo. Corporations often need owner approval, a bylaw, or clear procedural rules before using it for elections or motions.

## Key points

- Notice must explain how electronic voting works and how owners participate.
- Platforms should provide an audit trail for contested elections.
- Backup plans (proxy, in-person, or adjournment) matter if technology fails.

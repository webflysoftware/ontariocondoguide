## In plain language

An **owners' meeting** is where unit owners — not just the board — exercise collective authority. The most familiar example is the **AGM**, but owners may also meet for special business, requisitioned meetings, or turnover meetings.

Owners participate in person, by permitted remote means, or through a **proxy**. The corporation must give proper **notice** and achieve **quorum** before most binding votes.

## Types of owners' meetings

- **Annual general meeting (AGM)** — yearly meeting for reports, elections, and owner business
- **Special owners' meeting** — called for specific votes between AGMs
- **Requisitioned meeting** — called when owners meet statutory requisition thresholds
- **Turnover meeting** — first owner-controlled meeting after registration

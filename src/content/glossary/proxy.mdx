## In plain language

A **proxy** is how an Ontario condo owner says, "I cannot be there, but this person can vote for my unit." The proxy holder attends (or joins remotely if permitted), registers the unit, and votes according to the owner's instructions or within the authority granted on the form.

Proxies are central to **quorum** in many corporations because they count absent owners toward the participation threshold.

## Key points

- The corporation must include a proxy form in the **meeting notice** for owners' meetings.
- Proxies must meet Condominium Act requirements and your corporation's deadlines.
- Invalid proxies cannot be counted for quorum or voting.

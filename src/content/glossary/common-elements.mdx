## In plain language

**Common elements** are everything in the condo that is not inside your private unit — lobbies, elevators, hallways, roof, grounds, and often parking and recreational facilities. The **condominium corporation** owns and maintains them using **common expense** fees paid by owners.

The **declaration** defines unit boundaries and what counts as common element versus exclusive-use common element (e.g., a balcony assigned to one unit).

## Governance connection

The **board** decides many repair and service contracts affecting common elements. Major changes may require owner approval. Reserve fund planning focuses heavily on common element deterioration.

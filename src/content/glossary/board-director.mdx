## In plain language

A **board director** sits on the corporation's governing board. Directors set direction, approve budgets within their authority, hire and oversee management, and prepare **owners' meetings**. They owe **honest, good-faith** duties to the corporation — not to any single faction of owners.

Directors are usually **elected at an AGM** for terms set in the bylaws. Vacancies may be filled by the board or owners depending on the documents.

## Key responsibilities

- Financial oversight and reserve fund stewardship
- Conflict of interest disclosure
- Consistent enforcement of governing documents
- Proper meeting process and accurate minutes

## In plain language

When you buy a condo unit in Ontario, you also become part of a **condominium corporation**. The corporation is a separate legal person — it can enter contracts, hold bank accounts, sue and be sued, and own the **common elements** (lobbies, hallways, roof, etc.).

Owners govern the corporation through the **board of directors** and **owners' meetings**. Day-to-day operations are usually delegated to **management**.

## Key documents

- **Declaration** — foundational document defining units and ownership interests
- **Bylaws** — governance rules for meetings, board, and administration
- **Rules** — day-to-day conduct standards for owners and residents

## In plain language

**Quorum** answers the question: "Are enough owners represented to make this vote legitimate?" Without it, an Ontario condo meeting generally cannot elect directors, approve budgets, or pass owner-level motions.

Each **voting unit** counts once toward quorum. **Proxies** count if valid. The required percentage is set by the **Condominium Act** and your **declaration** or **bylaws** — often 25% of units, but verify for your corporation.

## How participation is counted

| Counts toward quorum | Usually does not |
| --- | --- |
| Owner present in person | Guest without a unit |
| Valid proxy for a unit | Invalid or late proxy |
| Remote owner if permitted | Tenant without voting rights |
| One co-owner attending for the unit | Same unit counted twice |

## If quorum fails

The chair typically **adjourns** the meeting. The corporation may reconvene on a second date stated in the notice or issue new notice. Repeated failures delay elections and owner decisions — see [what happens if quorum is not met](/guides/what-happens-if-quorum-not-met).

## Quorum vs voting majority

Quorum is **not** the same as winning a vote. You need quorum to hold the vote; separate rules determine whether a motion passes (e.g., majority of those present and voting).

Virtual and hybrid meetings are now a normal option for Ontario condominium corporations — not a temporary workaround. When done well, online AGMs and owners' meetings can improve access for snowbirds, busy owners, and residents who cannot easily attend in person. When done poorly, they create the same disputes as bad in-person meetings, plus technology failures owners never forget.

This guide explains what virtual meetings mean in Ontario condos, how to plan them, and where electronic participation fits. It is educational information only, not legal advice.

## What this means

A **virtual owners' meeting** is one where owners participate remotely through a video or audio platform rather than gathering in a physical room. A **hybrid meeting** combines in-person attendance with remote access so owners can choose either option.

Virtual meetings still must satisfy the same core requirements as in-person meetings:

- Proper **notice** with date, time, and instructions for joining
- Valid **quorum** based on units represented
- Lawful **voting** on properly noticed business
- Accurate **minutes** and follow-up

The **Condominium Act, 1998** and amendments over recent years have expanded options for electronic participation and voting, but **your declaration and bylaws** still govern what your corporation may do. Some corporations need owner-approved bylaws before electronic voting or fully virtual meetings are available for elections and other binding votes.

For AGM fundamentals, see our [Ontario condo AGM guide](/guides/ontario-condo-agm-guide). For notice content when adding virtual access, see [meeting notice requirements](/guides/ontario-condo-meeting-notice).

## When it matters

Virtual format matters when:

- **Turnout is chronically low** and distance or scheduling is a barrier
- **A large corporation** cannot fit all owners in one party room
- **Owners are dispersed** — snowbirds, investors, or units used seasonally
- **Health or accessibility** concerns make in-person attendance difficult
- **The corporation wants clearer voting records** than paper proxies alone provide

Virtual meetings also matter when boards want to reduce [proxy warfare](/guides/proxy-warfare-ontario-condo-voting) — organized paper proxy campaigns thrive where participation is opaque. Verified electronic participation can improve audit trails when implemented properly.

They are not automatic upgrades. Without bylaws, testing, and owner education, a virtual AGM can fail on quorum or split owners who cannot connect.

## Who is involved

| Role | Virtual meeting role |
| --- | --- |
| **Board** | Approves format, platform choice, and voting method; ensures documents allow it |
| **Manager / IT support** | Hosts platform, manages registration, troubleshoots access |
| **Chair** | Controls floor, recognizes speakers, declares quorum from combined attendance |
| **Scrutineer** | Oversees electronic and in-person votes consistently |
| **Owners** | Register in advance, test links, vote through approved channels |
| **Platform vendor** | May provide voting, identity checks, and participation logs |

Owners who struggle with technology may still use **proxies** or in-person attendance at hybrid meetings. Plan accommodation rather than assuming every owner will join by app.

## Typical process

### 1. Confirm authority for virtual format

Review the Act, your declaration, and bylaws. Identify whether you need a bylaw amendment or owner vote before electronic voting or fully virtual meetings. Seek professional guidance for contested interpretations.

### 2. Choose platform and voting method

Select a platform that supports your meeting size, Q&A, and voting needs. Understand how identity is verified and how votes are recorded. Align with any digital voting policies your corporation adopts under its governing documents.

### 3. Integrate into AGM preparation

Add virtual steps to your [AGM preparation timeline](/guides/how-to-prepare-condo-agm):

- Registration list tying unit numbers to participants
- Pre-meeting tech instructions in the notice package
- Backup dial-in or adjournment plan if the platform fails

### 4. Issue notice with virtual details

Notice must include **clear join instructions** — link, dial-in, deadlines to register, and how proxies or electronic votes work. Vague "link to follow" language causes no-shows and quorum problems.

### 5. Track quorum across channels

Combine in-person units, valid proxies, and verified electronic participants. Use the [quorum calculator](/tools/quorum-calculator) during the registration period, not only at call to order.

### 6. Run the meeting with dual visibility

The chair must see both room and screen. Manage mute, speaking order, and voting windows so remote owners are not second-class participants.

### 7. Document and retain records

Keep platform attendance logs, vote exports, and minutes that show how quorum and results were determined.

Run the [AGM readiness check](/tools/agm-readiness-check) with virtual-specific items added from the [AGM checklist](/templates/agm-checklist).

## Common mistakes

Virtual meetings introduce familiar governance errors plus new ones:

- **Skipping bylaw review** — holding electronic votes without authority
- **Weak notice instructions** — owners cannot join on time
- **No registration control** — unauthorized attendees or duplicate participation
- **Quorum miscounts** — forgetting remote units in the total
- **Untested technology** — platform limits exceeded or hosts unprepared
- **Excluding less tech-savvy owners** — no proxy or in-person alternative
- **Ignoring privacy** — recording or sharing participant data improperly

Many overlap with [common AGM mistakes](/guides/common-condo-agm-mistakes). Treat virtual meetings with the same seriousness as in-person process, plus a technology rehearsal.

## Checklist

Before a virtual or hybrid owners' meeting:

- [ ] Declaration and bylaws reviewed for electronic participation and voting
- [ ] Board-approved platform and voting method selected
- [ ] Owner registration process defined and communicated
- [ ] Notice includes join links, dial-in, time zone, and support contact
- [ ] Proxy and electronic vote instructions consistent with governing documents
- [ ] Quorum tracking covers in-person, proxy, and remote units
- [ ] Tech rehearsal completed with chair, manager, and scrutineer
- [ ] Backup plan documented if platform fails mid-meeting
- [ ] Accessibility options available (phone-in, proxy, in-person seat)
- [ ] Attendance and vote records retention plan confirmed
- [ ] [AGM readiness check](/tools/agm-readiness-check) completed with virtual items

Virtual meetings can make Ontario condo governance more inclusive and transparent — but only when the corporation has authority to hold them, owners know how to participate, and the board treats the online room with the same procedural care as the physical one.

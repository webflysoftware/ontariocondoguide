     Article Idea: "Proxy Warfare: How Outdated Voting Rules Are Undermining Ontario Condo Democracy — And What Modern Tech Can Fix"
Why This Is Interesting
Condo board elections in Ontario are ripe for drama. Low owner turnout, proxy form battles, allegations of manipulation, and stalled AGMs create perfect storm conditions for conflict, legal disputes, and even "hostile takeovers" by organized factions. Recent discussions (including calls to abolish or heavily reform proxy voting under the Condominium Act) show this is a live, contentious issue affecting thousands of owners.
Angle & Hook
Frame it as a governance thriller mixed with practical reform:

Start with a real or anonymized case study (e.g., disputed elections leading to CAT applications, court battles over proxies, or corps without AGMs/elections for years).
Highlight systemic problems: Proxy fraud risks, biased scrutineers/chairs, difficulty achieving quorum, apathy enabling control grabs, and how paper proxies create unverifiable chaos.
Contrast with the promise of secure electronic voting (already partially enabled but underused), including better security, accessibility for snowbirds/absent owners, audit trails, and higher participation.

Structure Outline

The Problem (Hook + Data): Stats on low turnout, examples of proxy disputes, financial/human costs (legal fees, delayed repairs, bad decisions).
Real-World Stories: Ontario-specific examples of elections gone wrong (takeovers, invalidations, manager influence concerns).
The Legal Landscape: Quick breakdown of the Condominium Act rules on proxies, quorums, notices, and director qualifications — plus gaps that fuel disputes.
The Tech Solution: How modern platforms (with features like verified identity, real-time auditing, hybrid meetings, and anti-fraud measures) address these pain points. Tie in broader trends from other jurisdictions that moved away from proxies.
Actionable Advice: For owners (how to get involved or spot red flags), boards (best practices for fair elections), and corps (considering bylaw updates for e-voting).
Future Outlook: Potential Act amendments, benefits for transparency/reserve funding decisions, and why better elections lead to better communities.
